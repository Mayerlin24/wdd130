Home page

Home Rosario is a cosmopolitan city located in the province of Santa, Fe, Argentina. Known as the birthplace of the flag has a historical cultural heritage. The National Flag Memorial (Monumento Nacional a la Bandera) is one of the heritage stands as a symbol of the city to remember the creation of the Argentina flag. In addition, you will be able to find different places to enjoy its cultural variety because the city has several theaters, art galleries, and museums. However, one of the principal attractions in Rosario, Argentina is the waterfront in front of the Paraná River (la Costanera) then here you will be able to find many water attractions to enjoy in the different seasons of the year. Overall, Rosario is a dynamic and culturally rich city with a vibrant arts scene, beautiful riverfront areas, and a deep-rooted passion for sports. Whether you are interested in history, or culture, or simply enjoying the lively atmosphere, Rosario has something to offer for every visitor.

Waterfront (Costanera)

The waterfront in Rosario is known for its social and cultural scene. During weekends and holidays, it often becomes a gathering place for locals and tourists alike. People gather along the riverfront to socialize, enjoy street performances, and participate in various events and festivals that take place in the area. The waterfront is also home to several outdoor art installations and sculptures, adding to the artistic atmosphere of the area. These installations provide opportunities for visitors to engage with public art and appreciate the creativity of local and international artists. Furthermore, the waterfront of Rosario is a perfect spot to witness beautiful sunsets over the Paraná River. As the sun goes down, the colors reflect on the water, creating a breathtaking spectacle that attracts photographers and nature enthusiasts. Overall, the waterfront of Rosario offers a dynamic and inviting atmosphere where people can connect with nature, explore cultural landmarks, engage in recreational activities, and immerse themselves in the city's vibrant social life.

Places

Parks

Rosario, Argentina is renowned for its beautiful parks and green spaces that offer residents and visitors opportunities for recreation and relaxation. The main parks are Independence Park, Spain Park, Allem Park, and Urquiza Park in those parks you can have an escape from the bustling city, providing benches and shaded areas where visitors can relax, read a book, or enjoy a picnic. Normally many people enjoy these activities in summer, however when in winter there is a sunny day you can see the parks full of these types of activities. So if you love the summer or winter you will be able to enjoy those activities. Likewise, there are children's parks near the other parks, for example near Independence Park you will find the children's garden (El Jardín de los Niños).

Football (soccer)

Football (soccer) If you know an Argentine, you know the passion for soccer. Rosario is the cradle of the best of all time Leonel Messi. The city is home to two major football clubs, Rosario Central and Newell's Old Boys, which have a fierce rivalry and a dedicated fan base. Football matches in Rosario are known for their intense atmosphere and passionate supporters.

Collectivities

Due to immigration in Rosario, there is a great cultural variety. If you decide to come in November you will be able to enjoy the Night of Collectivities, with the participation of more than twenty foreign communities associations that show stands the typical things of their country such as dances, arts, and several typical dishes of food. This activity lasts 10 days in which you can enjoy the culture of different countries. So if you are a tourist and your country has a stand you could feel at home.
